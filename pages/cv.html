Athabasca University

Cum Laude(2024)
MA (Interdisciplinary Studies)

This capstone project uses a grounded theory approach to develop an interdisciplinary feminist understanding of why women disproportionately engage with true crime content, drawing on insights from gender studies, communications, media studies, and psychology. It argues that women are drawn to true crime for its perceived educational value - particularly around safety, survival, and navigating justice systems - as well as for the solidarity and validation found within its associated communities. The project further proposes that these communities serve as sites where women can challenge dominant narratives of victimhood and collaboratively construct counter-discourses grounded in feminist politics.
